About me

Experienced, thoughtful, and genuinely invested in how people learn.

I hold a double major bachelor’s degree in Linguistics and German Studies, a minor in Spanish, and a Master’s degree in German Studies. My graduate work included Arizona teaching certification, and I was recognized by the Dean of Humanities as an Outstanding Graduate Assistant in Teaching.

I have taught German at the university level from 101 through 202, spent a semester teaching at the high school level, and have extensive experience with private instruction. During graduate school, I also taught intensive intermediate German for American university study abroad participants at the University of Leipzig for two summers in a row, where I worked closely with PhD students and professors.

I now teach adults in Berlin in both one-on-one and small-group settings, with lessons shaped by real learner goals, clear explanation, and a strong feel for how language works.
